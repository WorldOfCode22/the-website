From Biology to Web Development

When I was in my 6th semester of college (2nd semester Junior year) I took a deep look at my lives path at the time. I had spent three years in college. During that time I had not really been excited to learn 90% of the biology I was taught. I had my main focus in genetics and Bioinformatics. These were graduate level studies. At the end of my Junior year, it felt like all I had really been taught was theory and how to follow protocols on a sheet. Even common things like Gram stains we only did once or twice so I never had an opportunity to actually learn practical lab skills. Between not learning skills, limited opportunities in my area of interest and changing interest I felt it was time for a change in my life.

During the peak of my frustration and aggravation at the situation, I was driving back to Winthrop from a weekend at home. I had youtube playing while I was driving and I came across a video that was comparing self-taught, code camp, and college as methods of learning to program. I always had assumed that one needed a computer science degree and a heavy math background. This video showed me that these were things that were helpful but not absolutely needed. That day (October 1, 2017) I went to one of the websites the video suggested, freecodecamp.org, and started my journey into web development.
